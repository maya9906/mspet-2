Winter white hamsters are the most common species of dwarf hamsters in Korea, also known as jungle hamsters and Russian hamsters. The fur color is diverse, including normal, pearl, pearl, pudding, and blue sapphire. Normal is gray-brown fur with black stripes on the back all year round, pearls are white fur with black stripes on the back, sapphires are light gray, and pudding is yellow. Winter white hamsters have an average body length of 10 cm and a tail length of about 1 cm, but their tails are buried in their fur and are hard to see. There are stripes on the back. The eyes are big, the ears are small, and the hair is dense. The lifespan is 18 to 24 months. It's a hamster from a cold region, but it doesn't hibernate.
